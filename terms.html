4. How Init Works

4.1 Interest Expression

Init allows you to express interest in other people across six categories: Casual Date, Love, Marriage, Apology, Business, and Friendship. You may express interest by providing a target person's phone number, email address, username, or linked social media profile.

4.2 Privacy-Preserving Matching

Your interest expressions are private and invisible to others unless the interest is mutual. This is a fundamental design principle of Init.

  • When you express interest in someone, they are not notified.
  • Only when the other person independently expresses interest in you — with at least one overlapping category — are both parties notified of the mutual match.
  • Non-mutual interests are never revealed to any party.

4.3 Post-Match Communication

Once a mutual match is detected, both users can communicate through an in-app messaging system. Messaging is only available between matched users.

4.4 Social Link Verification

You may link social media accounts to your profile. Certain platforms require verification: phones and emails are verified via OTP; Instagram is verified via the Code-in-Bio method. Linking a social account that you do not own is a violation of these Terms.
